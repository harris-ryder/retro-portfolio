Making masks for the NHS

2020-09-18

At the start of Covid-19, PPE shortages left hospital staff without basic protection. I proposed a solution to the head of my company: a small investment to procure 3D printers, adapt an existing face mask design, and run a production line.

There were multiple 3D designs out there. I found 3DVerkstan´s design to be the most optimal, in terms of material use and output.

I spent days increasing output by trying different print settings. Such as print arrangements on the bed, trying to reduce unproductive nozzle movement. Thinning out the actual product design.

I also added custom GCODE so the printer could automatically remove the print from the bed and start the next one. This doubled output. No human required, the printers ran all night.

We produced over 2000 masks, we sold 1500 to the NHS and donated 500+ to care homes.
